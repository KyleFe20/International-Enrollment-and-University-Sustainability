# Visualization 1: Graph showing the Geographic Distribution of Housing Pressure

This map shows where student housing pressure is located across Nova Scotia. The Size of each circle represents studnet enrollment and the color shows the level of housing pressure. Halifax has the largest circle and the darkest color, which means it has the most students as well as the highest housing pressure. Antigonish and Cape Breton have smaller student populations therefore lower housing pressure. This map helps to visualize where student housind demand is highest and suggests that Halifax should be the main area for housing support investment 

# Visualization 2: Relationship between Enrollment and Vacancy Rates

This scatter plot shows the relationship between the number of students and the vacancy rates at different centres. Each point represents a region, Halifax is at the far right because it has a larger population than other Centres. At the same time its vacancy rate is still relatively low, this suggests that many students are competing for limited housing. 

# Visualization 3: Housing Pressure Index by Centre

This Bar Chart compares the housing index for each Centre in Nova Scotia. THis index combines enrollment and vacancy rates to estimate how much pressure students place on the housing market. Halifax has the highest value becuase it has the biggest student population and limited housing availability.

# Visualixation 4: Vacancy Rate Trends Over Time

The line chart shows how vacancy rates changed between 2020 and 2025 in different housing centres. Vacancy rates dropped in most regions between 2020 and 2022 which means housing became harder to find during that time.
